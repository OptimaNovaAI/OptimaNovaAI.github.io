Session Setup: Critical Thought Partner
▾
You are a senior technical advisor helping me develop transportation planning deliverables for the Metropolitan Washington Council of Governments. Your audience expertise spans first-year civil engineering students to 30-year FHWA/FTA executives. Your job is to be an objective, critical thought partner. Do not agree with me by default. If my framing is too technical, flag it. If my logic has a gap, call it out. If there is a better approach, tell me directly. Ask clarifying questions before beginning any task.
💡 Paste this at the start of any major work session. It sets expectations for the entire conversation.

Context Loader: CMP Technical Report
▾
I am the Program Manager for the Transportation Planning Board's Congestion Management Process. I am building a technical report as an ArcGIS Story Map with interactive web maps and Power BI dashboards. The report covers 10 core congestion metrics, uses data from the RITIS Probe Data Analytics Suite and NPM RDS Analytics Suite, and must comply with federal CMP requirements from both FHWA and FTA. My audiences include: TPB subcommittee members, senior MPO leadership, federal agency executives, consulting firms with 30+ years of experience, and people encountering congestion data for the first time. All writing must be accessible without being condescending.
💡 Load this context so the AI understands your program without you re-explaining it every time.

The Clarifying Questions Hack
▾
Ask any clarifying questions you have before beginning this task.
💡 Add this to the end of every single prompt. The AI will ask you things you didn't think to include, filling gaps automatically. This one line transforms every interaction.

Audience Calibration: Mixed Readership
▾
I need to write a section about [METRIC NAME]. Draft two versions: (1) A technical version appropriate for senior FHWA/FTA officials and experienced transportation consultants. (2) An accessible version appropriate for someone encountering this concept for the first time. Then write a final merged version that serves both audiences without condescending to either. The merged version should lead with clarity, layer in technical precision, and define specialized terms naturally within the text. Ask any clarifying questions before beginning this task.
💡 Replace [METRIC NAME] with any of your 10 core metrics. This gives you three options to work from.

Jargon Translator

Translate this concept into plain language that is accurate, accessible, and not patronizing. Include a one-sentence definition, followed by a real-world analogy that makes the concept intuitive. Data Interpretation to Narrative
▾
Here are my summary statistics for [METRIC NAME]: Mean: [X], Median: [Y], Std Dev: [Z]. The median is [higher/lower] than the mean, indicating [skew direction]. Write a 2-3 paragraph discussion section that: (1) Explains what these statistics mean in practical transportation terms. (2) Highlights why the difference between mean and median matters for this metric. (3) Frames the finding in language accessible to a non-planner while remaining technically accurate. (4) Suggests what this means for congestion in the TPB region specifically. Ask any clarifying questions before beginning this task.
💡 Feed in your actual numbers. This is the "stats to story" converter.

Parameter Validation
▾
I am about to download data from [TOOL NAME] with the following parameters: [LIST YOUR PARAMETERS]. My goal is to analyze [DESCRIBE OBJECTIVE] for the TPB region. Review these parameters and tell me: (1) Are they correct for my objective? (2) Should any parameter be changed? If so, which one and why? (3) Are there any parameters I am missing that would improve my analysis? Do not agree with my choices by default. If something should be changed, say so directly. Ask any clarifying questions before beginning this task.
💡 The last line prevents the AI from rubber-stamping your settings.
■
Data Quality Check
▾
I have formatted my data for [METRIC NAME]. Before I proceed to analysis, review the following sample and flag any issues with formatting, missing values, outliers, or inconsistencies that could affect my results. [PASTE DATA SAMPLE OR DESCRIBE FORMAT]. Ask any clarifying questions before beginning this task.
💡 Run this before analysis. Cheaper to fix issues now than after building visualizations.

These prompts solve the "script rewriting" problem by constraining the AI to targeted, minimal edits.

❯
Single Line Fix (No Rewrite)
▾
I have a Python script that is working correctly except for one issue on line [LINE NUMBER]. The issue is: [DESCRIBE THE PROBLEM]. Here is the relevant code block: [PASTE 5-10 LINES AROUND THE ISSUE]. Give me ONLY the replacement line. Do not rewrite the full script. Do not add new functions or restructure the code. Just tell me what line [LINE NUMBER] should say instead.
💡 This is the guardrail you needed. "Do not rewrite the full script" prevents the AI from going rogue.
❯
Code Review (Not Rewrite)
▾
Review the following Python script for bugs, inefficiencies, or improvements. Do not rewrite the script. Instead, provide a numbered list of specific issues with: (1) The line number. (2) What the issue is. (3) The exact fix for that line only. If the script is clean, say so. Do not suggest stylistic changes unless they affect functionality. [PASTE SCRIPT]
💡 Code review without the AI taking over your codebase.
❯
R to Python Translation
▾
I have the following R script that I need to convert to Python. Translate it line by line, preserving the logic and structure. Use pandas for data manipulation and matplotlib/seaborn for visualization where applicable. Add comments explaining any differences between the R and Python approaches. [PASTE R CODE]. Ask any clarifying questions before beginning this task.
💡 Learn the Python equivalents of what you already know in R.

Core principles that improve every AI interaction, regardless of which tool you use.

Be specific.
The more detail you provide, the better the output. Include your audience, format, length, tone, and constraints. Think of it like onboarding a new team member: they need the details to do good work.
Always end with "Ask any clarifying questions before beginning this task."
This one habit will improve every single interaction. The AI fills in the gaps you didn't know you had.
Show it what an A+ looks like.
Paste an example of a finished report, a well-written section, or a clean script. Say: "This is the standard. Everything you produce should mirror this quality and format."
Set the role up front.
Tell the AI who it is: "You are a senior transportation planning advisor." This frames every response for the rest of the session.
Ask: "What do you need from me to do this well?"
Flip the relationship. Let the AI tell you what context it is missing. You will be surprised what it asks for.
Do not settle for the first output.
Iterate. Say: "This is close but too technical in paragraph 2. Simplify without losing accuracy." Treat it like a draft review with a colleague.
Constrain the output.
Say: "Do not exceed 3 paragraphs. Use no more than 2 bullet points. Keep it under 200 words." Constraints produce better writing.
Shift from doer to reviewer.
Let AI take the first pass. Your job is to review, refine, and approve. Execution is deferred. Judgment stays with you.
